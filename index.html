WHAT IS GEOSPATIAL ENGINEERING?

Not familiar with geospatial engineering? Here's a quick interactive introduction to my field of work!

Satellite Data

We use data from satellites orbiting Earth to monitor environmental changes.

Like a doctor uses X-rays to see inside the body, we use satellite images to see Earth's health from space.

Mapping & Analysis

We create intelligent maps that help understand complex environmental patterns.

Think of Google Maps, but with superpowers to predict floods, track deforestation, and monitor climate change.

AI Solutions

We train computers to automatically detect environmental changes from satellite data.

Similar to facial recognition, but for Earth's features - identifying floods, droughts, and urban growth patterns.

Why It Matters

Geospatial engineering helps solve some of the world's most pressing challenges:

  • Predicting natural disasters before they happen
  • Monitoring climate change impacts in real-time
  • Optimizing renewable energy deployment
  • Protecting endangered ecosystems
  • Improving urban planning for sustainable cities

Real-World Example

When a flood occurs, our systems can automatically detect the affected areas from satellite images within hours, helping emergency responders save lives and allocate resources efficiently.

That's what I work on!
